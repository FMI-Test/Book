# Policy Doctrine: Rules of Engagement (RoE)

![Policy Doctrine: Rules of Engagement (RoE)](images/RULES-OF-ENGAGEMENT.webp)

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2026-03-03
**Status:** Draft

---

## 1.0 Overview & Purpose

This document establishes the official Rules of Engagement (RoE) governing all operational activities. The purpose of this RoE is to provide a clear, unambiguous framework for decision-making, ensuring that all actions are decisive, efficient, and aligned with strategic objectives.

The core principle is **"Precision and Proportionality."** We do not engage in indiscriminate, high-friction activities. Every action must be calculated, targeted, and justified by a clear strategic benefit that outweighs the cost.

## 2.0 Core Principles

These three principles are the foundation of this RoE and must inform every operational decision.

1.  **Strategic Intent:** All actions must directly serve a validated strategic objective. "Busy work," speculative engagements, and actions without a clear link to the mission are prohibited.
2.  **Economic Efficiency:** The "cost-to-benefit" ratio of any engagement must be a primary consideration. We prioritize low-cost, high-impact actions. Engagements that incur significant resource drain for minimal gain are to be avoided.
3.  **Frictionless Execution:** The "Kill Chain" must be optimized for speed and efficiency. Bureaucratic delays, excessive layers of approval, and any form of operational friction are considered critical failures. The default posture is to empower operators at the edge to act decisively within these rules.

## 3.0 Engagement Criteria: The "Green Light" Checklist

An operator is authorized to engage a target if, and only if, all of the following criteria are met:

-   **[ ] Positive Identification (PID):** The target is identified with 100% certainty. There is no room for ambiguity.
-   **[ ] Hostile Intent Verified:** The target has demonstrated clear hostile intent or is a declared hostile actor operating within a pre-defined hostile network.
-   **[ ] Strategic Value Confirmed:** The target represents a high-value component of a hostile infrastructure. Its removal will cause a disproportionately large disruption to the adversary's operations.
-   **[ ] Low Collateral Risk:** The engagement can be executed with minimal to zero collateral damage or unintended secondary effects.
-   **[ ] Favorable Cost-Benefit:** The resources required to prosecute the target are minimal compared to the strategic value of its removal.

If any of these criteria are not met, the engagement is **NOT AUTHORIZED.**

## 4.0 Prohibited Actions & De-escalation

To maintain operational integrity and strategic focus, the following actions are explicitly prohibited:

-   **Engaging Non-Hostile Targets:** Actions against neutral, civilian, or unverified targets are forbidden.
-   **Retaliatory Escalation:** Engagements must not be driven by emotion or retaliation. All actions must be strategic, not reactive. If an engagement risks a strategically disadvantageous escalation, it must be aborted.
-   **"Scorched Earth" Tactics:** Indiscriminate actions that cause widespread, non-specific damage are counter to the principle of Precision and Proportionality.

## 5.0 Post-Engagement Protocol

Immediately following any engagement, the following protocol is mandatory:

1.  **Report:** A concise After-Action Report (AAR) must be filed, detailing the target, justification for engagement (cross-referencing the "Green Light" checklist), resources used, and observed outcome.
2.  **Analyze:** All engagement data is to be analyzed to refine and improve the "Kill Chain" process, update threat intelligence, and measure strategic impact.
3.  **Reset:** Return to a state of readiness and continue monitoring for the next high-value opportunity.

This RoE is not a static document. It is a living framework that will be updated based on the analysis of operational data to ensure continuous improvement and adaptation. All personnel are expected to know and operate within these rules at all times.
