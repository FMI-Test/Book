## 5) Backward-tree research: parents, grandparents, and origins

To go backward from Aliyar Fakhran, the highest-value questions are:

- who exactly were **his father and mother**;
- whether the line connects to older **Garmeh / Jajarm / Bojnourd** administrative or landholding families;
- maternal vs paternal surname and title usage before **Fakhran** became the stable family name;
- which titles were honorific only and which indicate actual office, land, or lineage;
- whether surviving documents exist in:
  - Iranian MFA archives,
  - local civil registry records,
  - family-held certificates or inscriptions,
  - old photographs or gravestone records.

## 6) “1000 Families” context — where the Fakhran story may fit

The phrase **"Hezâr Fâmil" / “1000 Families”** in Iranian discourse was not a literal census. It was a shorthand for the **interconnected elite families** of the late Qajar / Pahlavi state: court households, landholders, ministers, officers, financiers, and people with access to state machinery.

For the Fakhran material, the useful question is not simply, *“Were they one of the 1000 Families?”* The better question is:

> **What kind of provincial-national elite position did the family occupy, and how did that position evolve across Qajar, Pahlavi, and post-1979 eras?**

That keeps the work analytical rather than mythic.

---

## 7) Pahlavi, post-1979, diaspora, and corporate-era continuity

A strong dynasty note should track not just noble titles, but also **institutional continuity**:

- Qajar / early Pahlavi: diplomacy, governorship, court, justice, local administration
- later Pahlavi: medicine, banking, bureaucracy, regional prestige, elite education
- post-1979: disruption, diaspora, adaptation, corporate and technical careers abroad
- US/corporate era: architecture, banking, aerospace, consulting, AI, cloud, and international technical work

This matters because the family story is not only about ancestry. It is also about how an old administrative-diplomatic line may have **reconfigured itself under modern institutions**.

## 8) Legal and corporate research lanes to keep separate but organized

> **Not legal advice.** This is a research taxonomy only.

If the project later expands into family-business, property, banking, or cross-border legal history, keep those tracks **separate from the public biography** and document them by region:

| Region | Research lane |
| --- | --- |
| **Iran** | civil registry, probate, land, decrees, archives, honors, company history |
| **Georgia / Caucasus** | consular and diplomatic traces tied to Tbilisi / Batumi |
| **United States** | corporate history, employment/public records, probate, property, court separation |
| **Japan / China / Middle East** | only if later trade, corporate, or technology branches need documenting |
| **International** | archive permissions, publication rights, and cross-border citation rules |

The rule is simple: **history in the history file, litigation in the legal file, and corporate compliance in its own note if needed.**
